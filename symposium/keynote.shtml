Dr. James R. Lovvorn and Dr. Marjorie Brooks

The keynote speakers for the 2014 Symposium are SIUC's Dr. James R. Lovvorn and Dr. Marj Brooks. Both are faculty membwers in the College of Zoology, and actively involved in the universities Arctic Expedition (an interdisciplinary research project).

Dr. Lovvorn's research is interested in understanding the relationship between mobile top predators (birds) and their aquatic food webs. His group uses simulation models to link field and laboratory studies of the foraging of energetics of birds with the quality and dispersion of prey, and to predict prey availability as a function of food web interactions.

Marj Brooks' research is interested in linking the biogeochemical effects of multiple human stressors with the structure and function of aquatic comunities. She studies the species, population, and community level adaptions to dynamical changes in water geochemistry due to altered UV radiation, metal complexation, and varied flow regimes resulting from climate change. She also studies food web and detrital dynamics mediated by essential trace metals, fungal processing, and aquatic biogeochemistry across watersheds.
