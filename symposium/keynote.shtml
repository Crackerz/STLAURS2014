Dr. James R. Lovvorn and Dr. Marjorie Brooks




The keynote speakers for the 2014 Symposium are SIUC's Dr. James R. Lovvorn and Dr. Marj Brooks. As faculty members in the Department of Zoology within the College of Science, both scientists are actively involved in interdisciplinary research in the Arctic.

Dr. Lovvorn's research focuses on understanding the relationship between mobile top predators (birds) and their aquatic food webs. His group uses simulation models to link field and laboratory studies of the foraging of energetics of birds with the quality and dispersion of prey, and to predict prey availability as a function of food web interactions.

Dr. Brooks' research links the biogeochemical effects of multiple human stressors with the structure and function of aquatic ecosystems. She studies species-, population-, and community-level adaptations to dynamic changes in water geochemistry. Those changes occur due to chronic metals pollution and altered UV radiation from the early melting of sea ice with global warming.
